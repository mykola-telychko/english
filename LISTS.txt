George W. Bush's "Bullhorn Address" (2001)
While standing on rubble at Ground Zero after the 9/11 attacks, Bush's improvised remarks through a bullhorn ("I can hear you! The rest of the world hears you!") captured the national mood in a moment of crisis.

Theodore Roosevelt's "The Man with the Muck-Rake" (1906)
This speech coined a term for investigative journalists and helped define the Progressive Era's approach to corruption and reform.

Lyndon B. Johnson's "We Shall Overcome" (1965)
Johnson's speech to Congress urging passage of the Voting Rights Act adopted the anthem of the civil rights movement and represented a pivotal moment in civil rights legislation.

Barack Obama's "A More Perfect Union" (2008)
Obama's speech on race relations during his first presidential campaign addressed racial tensions in America with unusual candor and complexity for a political speech.
